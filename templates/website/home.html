Explore Cherrapunji

Seven Sister Falls

Explore Seven Sister Falls

The Seven Sisters Falls, also known as Nohsngithiang Falls, is one of Meghalaya’s most spectacular natural wonders. Located near Cherrapunji, this majestic waterfall is named after the seven streams that cascade down the limestone cliffs of the Khasi Hills, creating a mesmerizing sight.

Dawki River

Explore Dawki River

Nestled in the heart of Meghalaya, Dawki River is a breathtaking marvel known for its crystal-clear waters and picturesque surroundings. Flowing through the West Jaintia Hills, this river forms a natural border between India and Bangladesh. The most fascinating feature of the Dawki River is its glass-like transparency, creating an illusion that boats are floating in the air.

Cathedral of Mary

Explore Cathedral of Mary

Nestled in the heart of Shillong, the Cathedral of Mary Help of Christians is one of the most revered and architecturally stunning churches in Meghalaya. Built in 1936, this grand structure stands as a symbol of faith and heritage. The church is known for its beautiful stained-glass windows, high arches, and peaceful ambiance. Beneath the cathedral lies a grotto dedicated to Mother Mary, where visitors often come to offer prayers.

Meghalaya – Journey into the Heart of the Clouds

Meghalaya – the "Abode of Clouds" – is nature’s hidden jewel nestled in the lush hills of Northeast India. A land where every raindrop tells a story, where clouds gently kiss emerald-green valleys, and waterfalls tumble like poetry from the heavens. From the living root bridges of Cherrapunji and Mawlynnong, Asia’s cleanest village, to the shimmering Umiam Lake and the mysterious Mawsmai Caves, Meghalaya offers an experience like no other. Wander through misty trails, listen to ancient Khasi legends, and breathe in the serenity of landscapes untouched by time.
